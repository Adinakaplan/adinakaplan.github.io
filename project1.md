# Project 1: Proactive Risk Modeling for NYC Intersections

## Executive Summary
This project transitioned the NYC Department of Transportation from reactive safety (responding after a crash) to **proactive risk identification**. I developed a machine learning model to identify "Risk Signatures" at uncontrolled intersections that match the profile of high-crash locations, allowing the city to intervene before accidents occur.

---

## My Role: Machine Learning Architect
While collaborating with a team for data cleaning, I was specifically responsible for the **Predictive Modeling** and **Spatial Analysis** pipeline.

### 1. Technical Methodology: Random Forest Classifier
I implemented a **Random Forest Classifier** with 200 decision trees to identify complex, non-linear relationships between intersection infrastructure and crash frequency. This ensemble approach was chosen to prevent overfitting and ensure stable "risk signatures" across NYC’s vast intersection network.



### 2. High-Precision Spatial Engineering
To ensure administrative "Unknowns" in the data did not lead to safety gaps, I utilized the **EPSG:2263 (New York State Plane)** coordinate system. By using a flat grid measured in feet rather than standard latitude/longitude, I performed high-accuracy 50ft spatial joins to link over 100,000 crashes to specific physical infrastructure.

### 3. Metric Optimization: Prioritizing Recall
In public safety modeling, the cost of a "False Negative" (missing a dangerous intersection) is much higher than a "False Positive" (an unnecessary site audit). I tuned the model to prioritize **Recall (97%)**, ensuring that 97% of historically dangerous locations were successfully flagged for audit.

---

## Key Results & Findings
* **Predictive Confidence:** The model achieved a **0.9979 confidence score** in identifying sites matching the city’s highest-risk profiles.
* **Geographic Insights:** The analysis revealed that **87% of high-risk uncontrolled intersections** are concentrated in the **Bronx (55%)** and **Staten Island (32%)**.
* **Community Correlation:** I discovered that high-risk sites averaged over **61 citizen complaints**, proving a strong link between public reporting and infrastructure failure.
